You are examining ONE image of a scanned book page (or a portion of it) for
hand-drawn marks the reader added to highlight passages. Find EVERY mark — a
miss here cannot be recovered by any later step.

WHAT COUNTS AS A MARK
Structural marks (they bracket / underline a passage):
- Vertical lines, brackets, braces, curly shapes in EITHER margin
- Underlines spanning more than one word in the body text
Emphasis marks (they single a passage out as important — small and easy to
miss, so look hard for them):
- Exclamation mark(s) "!" or "!!", or a "?" in the margin
- Stars, arrows, ticks, checkmarks
- A squiggle, or a circle / loop drawn around words (an encircling)
- A word or comment written in the margin (e.g. "HA", "ROLL EYES")
- A doubled or wavy underline, or an underline laid over already-underlined text
Marks may be FAINT pencil — faint, thin, or short is still a mark.

HOW TO SCAN (be systematic — do not skim)
1. UNDERLINE SWEEP: read DOWN the body text line by line, all the way to the
   last line. Look for any thin horizontal line beneath words; pencil
   underlines are often very light and easily overlooked. An underline can run
   for many consecutive lines — do not miss a long run. Pay special attention
   to the LAST lines of the page: a mark there often continues onto the next
   page and is the easiest to miss.
2. MARGIN SWEEP: run down BOTH margins looking for the structural and emphasis
   marks above, including tiny ones — a single "!", a star, a tick. When you
   see a margin mark, say whether it looks like an EMPHASIS mark (!, star,
   arrow, squiggle, circle, comment) or a plain structural one (line, bracket).
3. Footnotes count: a hand-underline or margin mark on FOOTNOTE text is a real
   mark (only the printed footnote text / reference number itself is not).

WHAT IS NOT A MARK
- Printed punctuation, headers, page numbers, the printed footnote rule
- PRINTED ITALICS ARE NOT AN UNDERLINE. An underline is a separate hand-drawn
  line BENEATH the words; italics are slanted printed letters with NO line under
  them. Italic terms, book titles, and emphasized words are printed, not marked
  — never report italic type as an underline.
- Edge shadows uniform along the whole page edge; bleed-through (mirrored letters)
- A single underlined uncommon word with no other cue — vocabulary lookup, IGNORE
- Pure inference from interesting prose

Pages typically have 1–4 marks, sometimes more, sometimes none. Be exhaustive —
list every distinct mark you can see, faint ones included. Do not stop early.

OUTPUT FORMAT
For each mark, output one block:

MARK: <left margin / right margin / in-text> | <top / upper-middle / middle / lower-middle / bottom> | <shape: underline / vertical line / bracket / curly brace / exclamation / star / arrow / tick / circle / comment / ...>
EXTENT: <approx first and last words of the lines it spans, OR the underlined phrase verbatim>
NOTES: <faintness or uncertainty; whether it is an EMPHASIS mark (!, star, squiggle, circle, comment) vs a plain structural mark; whether it might be a vocabulary underline>

Separate marks with a blank line.
If after a thorough sweep you find NO marks, output exactly: NO MARKS
Output ONLY mark blocks (or NO MARKS). No preamble, no commentary.
