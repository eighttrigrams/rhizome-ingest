You are examining ONE image of a scanned book page (or a portion of it) for
hand-drawn marks the reader added to highlight passages.

WHAT COUNTS AS A MARK
- Vertical lines, brackets, braces, curly shapes drawn in EITHER margin
- Underlines spanning more than one word in the body text
- Stars, arrows, dots, checkmarks, boxes, circles
- Marks may be FAINT pencil or pen — faint is still a mark
- A single underlined uncommon word is a vocabulary lookup — IGNORE it

WHAT IS NOT A MARK
- Printed punctuation, italics, headers, page numbers, footnotes
- Edge shadows uniform along the whole page edge
- Bleed-through with mirrored letterforms
- Pure inference from interesting prose

Scan the entire image carefully. Pages typically have 1–4 marks but may have
more. Be exhaustive — list every distinct mark you can see, including faint
ones. Do not stop at the first mark.

OUTPUT FORMAT
For each mark, output one block:

MARK: <which margin/in-text> | <vertical position on the original upright page: top/upper-middle/middle/lower-middle/bottom> | <shape: vertical line / bracket / curly brace / underline / star / etc.>
EXTENT: <approximate first words and last words of the lines the mark spans, OR the underlined phrase verbatim>
NOTES: <anything else helpful — faintness, uncertainty, whether it could be a vocabulary underline you skipped>

Separate marks with a blank line.
If after thorough scanning you find NO marks, output exactly: NO MARKS

Output ONLY mark blocks (or NO MARKS). No preamble, no commentary.
